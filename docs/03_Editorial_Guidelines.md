# Editorial Guidelines

## Editorial identity

The Rugby Panda is an independent digital rugby newsroom.

It is not positioned publicly as an AI project.

## Public attribution

Articles should be signed:

**The Rugby Panda**

Do not use personal author names initially.

Preferred article metadata:

```text
Published 2 July 2026 • Updated 2 July 2026 • 6 min read • The Rugby Panda
```

## AI policy

AI may be used internally as a productivity tool for research, drafting, summarising and structuring.

Do not mention AI publicly unless legally or editorially necessary.

The public emphasis is on:
- accuracy
- independence
- editorial standards
- reader trust

## Editorial principles

Every article should answer at least one of:

1. What happened?
2. Why did it happen?
3. Why does it matter?

If an article answers none of these, do not publish.

## Standards

Avoid:
- clickbait
- rage-bait
- unverified rumours
- misleading headlines
- sensationalism
- empty filler content

Prioritise:
- accuracy
- clarity
- context
- insight
- fairness
- readability

## Tone

The Rugby Panda voice should be:
- intelligent
- clear
- calm
- analytical
- accessible
- independent

Not:
- overly emotional
- tribal
- inflammatory
- corporate
- generic

## Article signature feature

Every article should start with a **Key Points** section.

Purpose:
- help mobile readers understand quickly
- respect reader time
- establish The Rugby Panda as clear and useful

## Comments

No comments for v1.

Social discussion happens on Instagram/Facebook.

## Opinion

Opinion is a content type, not a top-level navigation item initially.

Opinion pieces still live under the relevant subject:
- Province
- Ireland
- URC
- Europe
